Chronology of Dr. Severo Ochoa

  • 1905 - Born in Luarca, Asturia (Spain).
  • 1912 - His father dies and he moves to Malaga to live with his mother.
  • 1922 - He moved to Madrid to study medicine.
  • 1928 - publishes his studies on creatinine in urine in the Journal of Biological Chemistry without being licensed.
  • 1929 - Get a degree in Medicine from the Complutense University of Madrid.
  • 1936 - He went into exile as a result of the Spanish Civil War. He continued research in Germany and later in the UK.
  • 1941 - Emigrates to the United States due to the beginning of World War II.
  • 1953 - He occupies a privileged position as a researcher at the New York University School of Medicine.
  • 1955 - Obtains US citizenship.
  • 1955 - Synthesizes RNA in the laboratory thanks to a suitable nucleotide substrate (the different letters that make up DNA and RNA, that is, their elementary components).
  • 1959 - Get the Nobel Prize in Physiology and Medicine for his studies on the synthesis of DNA and RNA.
  • 1971 - He creates the Molecular Biology Center in Spain, which bears his name.
  • 1979 - Receives the United States National Medal of Science.
  • 1986 - His wife Carmen García Cobián dies.
  • 1993 - With the title "The emotion of discovering" he publishes his biography.
  • 1993 - He dies at 88 years of age.
